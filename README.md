## Key Research Findings

### From the Data

1. **Physical + judgment = safe.** The safest jobs combine hands-on physical work with complex human judgment. Pure cognitive tasks are most at risk.

2. **The "AI-proof" skills**: empathy, physical dexterity, creative vision, crisis management, complex negotiation, ethical judgment.

3. **~9.3 million U.S. workers** are in high-risk occupations (70%+ automation risk). Source: NBER 2025.

4. **Women face 2x the risk**: 4.7% exposure for women vs 2.4% for men, largely due to concentration in clerical roles. Source: Brookings 2025.

5. **Education is protective**: 22% risk for workers without degrees vs 2% for university graduates. Source: OECD.

6. **Healthcare is the safest sector overall** — AI augments rather than replaces. Nurse practitioners projected to grow 52% by 2033. Source: BLS.

7. **Timeline acceleration**: Goldman Sachs estimates significant displacement within 5-7 years, not 10-15 as previously projected.

### What's Changed in 2026

- **Agentic AI** (OpenClaw, Claude, multi-step agents) is accelerating displacement of knowledge workers faster than physical workers
- **Voice AI agents** are replacing call center workers 2-3x faster than expected
- **AI coding tools** (Copilot, Cursor, Claude Code) are shifting junior developer roles but INCREASING senior developer demand
- **Creative AI** (Midjourney v7, Suno v5, Sora) is disrupting entry-level creative roles while creating new categories

## Methodology

Risk scores are composite estimates based on:

1. **Task automation potential** (40% weight) — What % of routine tasks in this occupation can current AI handle? Based on O*NET task databases and AI capability assessments.

2. **Historical displacement data** (20% weight) — How quickly have similar roles been automated in the past? BLS employment projections 2023-2033.

3. **AI capability trajectory** (20% weight) — How fast is AI improving in the specific capabilities this job requires? Based on AI benchmark trends.

4. **Structural barriers** (20% weight) — Licensing, regulation, liability, physical requirements, and social acceptance factors that slow automation even when technically possible.

**Limitations**: These are estimates, not predictions. Actual displacement depends on economic conditions, regulation, AI development pace, and industry adoption rates. Scores reflect 10-year risk windows.
